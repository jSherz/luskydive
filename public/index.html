Learn to skydive

The skydiving club subsidises training to enable you to become a licensed solo skydiver. There are two training options:

RAPS (Ram Air Progression)

Jump from 3,500ft and your parachute opens automatically via a static line attached to the plane. You then fly your parachute safely to the ground under instruction over a radio. After 5 static line jumps you progress to freefall where you deploy your own parachute and start to learn freefall skills like turns. On each jump you spend longer in freefall, jumping from increasing altitudes until you reach the max altitude of 13,500ft (with approximately 1 minute of freefall).

You can become a licensed skydiver in as little as 18 jumps, and then can go skydiving without an instructor! RAPS first jump courses consist of eight hours ground school and include two jumps at a cost of only £175. Every jump after that is just £36.

AFF (Accelerated Free Fall)

AFF starts from the maximum altitude so after after approximately 10 hours of ground school training, you jump from an awesome 13,500ft! Sound scary? Don't worry, you'll be jumping with two instructors holding onto you.

The first level consists of completing altitude checks and practicing deploying your parachute. Further levels (all the way up to level 8) build on your freefall skills and see you learning how to make turns, fly forwards through the sky and even do back flips!

After completing all eight levels, you will then do ten consolidation jumps that give you a chance to practice the skills you learnt during AFF. Completing these consolidation jumps, as well as some parachute flight exercises, earns you an A licence which identifies you as a qualified solo skydiver!

Because AFF is intensive and requires two instructors it is more expensive, costing £1200 for ground school and eight instructor levels then £330 for the ten consolidation jumps. If you want to try AFF it is £360 for ground school and level 1, then if you wish to carry on the remainder has to be paid in full.

NB: Whilst still learning to skydive, you do not need to pay the society kit hire.

Life after training

After qualifying, jump tickets go down to £19, and we're working on getting them even cheaper for you! Once you're licensed you can jump with your friends and the fun really begins.

Trips are run to the dropzone most weekends so you will have plenty of opportunities to skydive. We take part in the BCPA inter-university league and attend the six meets a year where there are formation skydiving, freeflying and accuracy competitions.

Kit

Thanks to Aerodyne Research and the university's fantastic footsteps fund, we have been able to secure three brand new rigs (parachute set-up) for the club. This investment will enable the society to grow and become even bigger and better! We're working hard to ensure that the kit is maintained to the highest standard and will continue to subsidise jump tickets even further.

These rigs are available for you to jump the minute you have achieved your full skydiving licence (in as little as 18 jumps)! Now there's even more of a reason to get qualified as quickly as possible!
